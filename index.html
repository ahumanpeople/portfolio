Intro to Solidworks · SFSU

Autonomous Drone CAD Project

Designed a fully-constrained quadrotor drone in Solidworks, including a custom-designed servo-driven gripper mechanism from scratch — modeled with assembly drawings suitable for machining.

Solidworks Assembly Design CAD Drawings BOM

This was the final project for my Intro to Solidworks course, where the objective was to design a quadrotor drone using 3D-printed material, with all parts modeled in Solidworks from scratch (except imported McMasterCarr parts). The project subject came from the challenge of reverse engineering a mock quad drone for an ASME IAM3D competition.

  • Max volume of 250 × 250 × 250 mm
  • Ease of assembly and easy repairability
  • All components neatly attached to the final assembly
  • Mechanism to pick up and set down a 1 × 1 × 1 in cube

The gripper mechanism was ideated and designed from scratch using a simple servo motor. Generated BOM, assembly drawings, an exploded view animation, and a propeller motion video.

Heat Transfer · SFSU

Transient Thermal Analysis of a Disc Brake

Compared thermal behavior of perforated vs. solid disc brakes using MATLAB simulations under three braking cases, investigating the impact of design choices and ambient conditions on performance and brake fade.

MATLAB Solidworks Thermal Analysis STL Modeling

This project tackled transient thermal behavior by predicting and simulating the behavior of disc brakes under different braking conditions. Our study compared a perforated disc brake with a solid disc brake to observe how design choices affect maximum temperature, cooling distribution, and overall efficiency.

  • Case I: Constant power applied using circular brake pads
  • Case II: Linearly decreasing power using ring-shaped brake pads
  • Case III: Linearly decreasing power using circular brake pads

Results showed the perforated disc achieved more even cooling distribution but reached higher max temperatures due to reduced conductive area. A parametric study revealed higher ambient temperatures increase brake fade risk, while colder/wet conditions enhance cooling but reduce friction.

Intro to Microcontrollers · SFSU

Smart Electric Kettle Attachment

Designed and built a microcontroller-based kettle attachment with a temperature probe, control buttons, and RGB LED — all housed in a custom 3D-printed lid modeled in Fusion360 to fit an existing stovetop kettle.

Arduino (C++) Fusion360 TinkerCAD 3D Printing Electronics

This was the final project for my Intro to Microcontrollers course — an opportunity to create a device that solved a real problem. My attachment replaces the stovetop kettle lid with a smart device that lets the user set a target temperature (depending on the drink desired) and alerts them via RGB LED when reached.

Originally planned as a drip coffee machine, repeated hardware failures (broken heating element, nonfunctional water sensor) prompted a pivot. The final device uses a DS18B20 temperature sensor, control buttons, RGB LED, and an Elegoo UNO R3, all housed in a Fusion360-designed, PLA-printed case perfectly sized to the original kettle lid.
